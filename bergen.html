Bergen, Norway — One Day Itinerary

Between the rain
and the fjord

Bergen is one of the rainiest cities in the world — a good day here means moving from wharf, to fortress, to mountaintop, with a warm stop between each.

Expect showers · bring a hood, not an umbrella
The Route

Nine stops, one day

09:00

Bryggen Wharf

Start at Bryggen, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Walk through the narrow wooden alleyways.

10:15

Bergenhus Fortress

Walk 5 minutes north to Bergenhus Fortress. Explore one of Norway's oldest stone fortifications. View the 13th-century Håkon's Hall and Rosenkrantz Tower.

11:00

St. Mary's Church

See St. Mary's Church, the oldest remaining building in Bergen.

12:00

Fish Market

Head over to the historic Fisketorget (Fish Market).

Try it: Brunost — Norwegian brown cheese ice cream at Vafleriet nearby.
1:30 PM

Summit Mount Fløyen

Walk to the nearby funicular station. Ride the Fløibanen Funicular up to the 320-meter summit in 6 minutes.

Tip: Book your Fløibanen Funicular tickets online in advance to skip the main ticket lines at the station.
2:00 PM

Mount Fløyen Viewpoint & Lake Walk

Take in panoramic views of the fjords and city. Stroll 10 minutes through the pine forest to the serene Skomakerdikket Lake.

4:00 PM

Skostredet & City Center

Walk down into Skostredet, Bergen's charming arts district filled with murals and boutique shops. Continue past Torgallmenningen Square.

8:30 PM

Twilight Harbor Walk

Finish your evening with a relaxed stroll along the illuminated Vågen harbor.

Late

Ice Bar

Cap off the night somewhere unmistakably cold.

If you have room to wander

Two nearby spots worth folding into the day.

Skomakerdiket (The Shoemaker's Dike)

A serene, picturesque mountain lake just a 10-minute walk from the top station of the Fløibanen funicular. Known for its flat, family-friendly gravel loop trail, it offers a peaceful forest retreat with modern amenities and spectacular natural views.

Byparken

A nice park in the centre of Bergen around the Lille Lungegårdsvannet — a natural lake later extended to its present dimensions — with a fountain at its center.
