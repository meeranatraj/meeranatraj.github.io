Bergen is one of the rainiest cities.

  1. 09:00 AM – Bryggen Wharf: Start at Bryggen, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Walk through the narrow wooden alleyways.

  1. 10:15 AM – Bergenhus Fortress: Walk 5 minutes north to Bergenhus Fortress. Explore one of Norway's oldest stone fortifications. View the 13th-century Håkon’s Hall and Rosenkrantz Tower.

  1. 11:00 AM – St. Mary’s Church: See St. Mary's Church, the oldest remaining building in Bergen.

  1. 12:00 PM – Fish Market: Head over to the historic Fisketorget (Fish Market).

**Brunost - Norwegian brown cheese ice cream at Vafleriet nearby.

  1. 1:30 PM – Summit Mount Fløyen: Walk to the nearby funicular station. Ride the Fløibanen Funicular up to the 320-meter summit in 6 minutes. Book your Fløibanen Funicular tickets online in advance to skip the main ticket lines at the station.

  1. 2:00 PM – Mount Fløyen Viewpoint & Lake Walk: Take in panoramic views of the fjords and city. Stroll 10 minutes through the pine forest to the serene Skomakerdikket Lake.

  1. 04:00 PM – Skostredet & City Center: Walk down into Skostredet, Bergen's charming arts district filled with murals and boutique shops. Continue past Torgallmenningen Square.

  1. 08:30 PM – Twilight Harbor Walk: Finish your night with a relaxed stroll along the illuminated Vågen harbor.

  1. Ice Bar.

Skomakerdiket (The Shoemaker's Dike) is a serene, picturesque mountain lake located just a 10-minute walk from the top station of the Fløibanen funicular in Bergen, Norway. Known for its flat, family-friendly gravel loop trail, it offers a peaceful forest retreat with modern amenities and spectacular natural view.

Byparken is a nice park in the centre of Bergen around the Lille Lungegårdsvannet – a natural lake later extended to its present dimensions – and a fountain in the middle of it.
